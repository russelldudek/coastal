Re: Vice President - Artificial Intelligence

Dear Coastal Hiring Team,

It seems the hardest part of this role is not introducing Coastal to AI. You already have strong signals in market: a trusted Salesforce position, Agentforce credibility, Waves for Agentic Ops, Data and AI services, an AI Operations research platform, and now the enterprise scale of TCS behind the firm. The more difficult question is how to turn those assets into one coherent AI practice that clients can understand, buy, trust, and grow with across Salesforce, OpenAI, Anthropic, and the wider data ecosystem.

That is why the role caught my attention. My career has been spent building where technology, operations, customers, and organizational behavior collide. I have led high-pressure operations at Amazon, advanced electronics and engineering programs at Compunetics, autonomous systems work through ZeusVu, and AI-first operating transformation at Vape-Jet. Through DudeWorth, Core, and DudexOS, I have also built AI readiness frameworks, agentic workflow concepts, advisory models, and practical operating patterns around LLMs, retrieval, human judgment, governance, and adoption.

My perspective is that Coastal does not need an AI practice organized around model demos. It needs a market position and service architecture that begin with client outcomes, create clear pathways from strategy to implementation to managed agentic operations, and help account teams tell a simple story about where Salesforce-native AI, multimodel enterprise AI, data modernization, and ongoing optimization each fit. The practice should make it easier for sales to open the right conversation, easier for clients to buy the next step, easier for delivery teams to succeed, and easier for leadership to know which bets are producing repeatable value.

The timing is unusually compelling. Coastal's own 2026 research says AI investment and confidence are high while results lag; its public materials show that adoption, data readiness, bandwidth, governance, and ongoing AI operations are the real friction. At the same time, OpenAI and Anthropic are deepening enterprise consulting ecosystems, large consultancies are scaling AI delivery programs, and model providers themselves are moving closer to deployment. Coastal's opportunity is distinctive: combine the trust and agility of a high-rated consultancy, deep Salesforce and data expertise, and TCS-backed delivery scale without becoming slow, generic, or model-bound.

I would approach the first chapter by listening across Coastal's executive, sales, marketing, alliances, delivery, innovation, and client teams; mapping the strongest current AI assets; defining a focused services portfolio and point of view; creating repeatable value-targeting and delivery guardrails; identifying a small number of industry and workflow beachheads; and co-selling the story with account teams until the market language and delivery model reinforce each other. Thought leadership should come from evidence, and internal enablement should make every client-facing leader more confident discussing where AI belongs—and where it does not.

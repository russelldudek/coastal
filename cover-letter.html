Dear Coastal Hiring Team,

It seems the risk in this search is finding one of two profiles: a brilliant AI technologist who has never had to build a market, or a polished consulting leader who can sell a vision but cannot interrogate the operating system behind it. My career has been built in the seam between those worlds.

I have spent more than twenty years turning complex technology into practical outcomes across advanced electronics, Amazon operations, autonomous systems, robotics, machine vision, customer workflows, and AI-first transformation. Through DudeWorth, Core, and DudexOS, I have also built AI readiness frameworks, agentic workflow patterns, advisory models, and operating concepts around LLMs, retrieval, human judgment, evaluation, governance, and adoption. The common thread is building from ambiguity, translating across technical and business teams, and making new technology useful enough to survive real work.

I see three jobs inside Coastal's VP of AI role. First, define the market signal: a clear point of view and services portfolio across Salesforce AI, OpenAI, Anthropic, and the wider data ecosystem. Second, build the delivery system: implementation standards, evaluation, governance, reusable methods, and a small high-performing engineering team. Third, make the company smarter about AI: equip sales, marketing, delivery, alliances, and internal operations with language, tools, and confidence they can use. Those three jobs should reinforce one another.

Coastal's timing is compelling. The firm already has customer trust, Agentforce credibility, proprietary AI Operations research, Waves for Agentic Ops, data and AI services, and TCS-backed scale. At the same time, Coastal's own research shows that investment and confidence remain high while results lag, adoption walls persist, and data and bandwidth constrain value. My thesis is that Coastal can own the space between AI strategy and reliable operation: help clients choose well, build wisely, drive adoption, and keep AI creating value after launch.
